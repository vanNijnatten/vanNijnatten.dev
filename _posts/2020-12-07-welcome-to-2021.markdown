Finally, 2020 is over. After moving to my new university and hospital, the year started quite well. Unfortunately I cannot really remember what happened in the first couple of months of 2020. I remember I was lonely because I knew nobody in this new city. I talked to my supervisor before as well as another medical student. However I did not know anybody and did not really have the chance to get to know my new collegues because I was (and am) the only bioinformatician in the room. Most people worked in the lab and came in once in a while.<!--more-->
I did try to get to know a couple of people. One PhD student in my room in particular, a new student from Colombia and a bioinformatician who I knew was not going to have an extension of his contract. I was supposed to take over his job to manage the HPC.

And then COVID-19 struck. I remember seeing it in the news, and I expected it to get over to our country in no time. And I expected the university to close down. And still that one day when we speculated on closing down the labs and having to work from home, kinda was a shock. About a week later, our prediction became reality.

From that moment on, all days blurred into each other.
